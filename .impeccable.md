# VividStack — Design Context

> **For AI agents:** This document defines the design identity for VividStack. Read it before making any visual, copy, or UX decisions. It is the single source of truth for brand personality, aesthetic direction, and design principles. Technical details below are aligned with the current repo (`tailwind.config.mjs`, `src/styles/global.css`, layouts, and data).

---

## Repository snapshot

**Stack:** [Astro](https://astro.build) 5.x (static output), TypeScript, Tailwind CSS 3.x via `@astrojs/tailwind` with `applyBaseStyles: false` (tokens and base styles live in `src/styles/global.css`). Motion: **GSAP** (ScrollTrigger, CustomEase) + **Lenis** smooth scroll. **Node** `>= 22.12.0`.

**Primary domain:** `https://vividstack.io` (canonical + Open Graph in `src/layouts/Layout.astro`).

**Routes:** `/` (marketing home), `/privacy`, `/terms`. Footer (`src/components/Footer.astro`) links to the legal pages with 44×44px minimum touch targets.

**Content source:** Section copy and portfolio metadata live in `src/data/home.ts` (selected works, advantages, services bento, process phases, capabilities grid).

**Home motion:** `src/scripts/home-motion.ts` owns GSAP/Lenis on `/` only. Script loads after **two** `requestAnimationFrame` ticks post-DOMContentLoaded to reduce main-thread contention. Desktop behavior uses viewport width `>= 1024px` at init (matches Tailwind `lg`); below that, heavy motion is skipped. **`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`** applies an instant, simplified end state (opacity-first; no floating/orbit-style motion).

**Contact:** Web3Forms client script is loaded on idle or first focus inside `#contact-form` (`Layout.astro` inline script) to keep the critical path small.

**Brand asset:** Default OG/social image pipeline uses `src/assets/Vivid-stack-logo.png` via `astro:assets`.

## Users

**Who they are:** Local business owners — restaurateurs, tradespeople, boutique retailers, service providers. They are **not** technical. They find VividStack through word of mouth today, and via Instagram and TikTok marketing in the near future.

**Their context:** They're shopping for a web presence that makes their business look legitimate, professional, and modern. Many have been burned by cheap freelancers or DIY website builders. They're comparing VividStack to other design agencies, not to other code repos.

**The job to be done:** "I need a website that makes my business look great, loads fast on my customers' phones, shows up on Google, and doesn't cost me a fortune every month to keep running."

**What they care about (in order):**
1. **Speed** — My site loads instantly, customers don't bounce
2. **Responsiveness** — Looks perfect on every phone and tablet (where their customers actually are)
3. **Reliability** — Never goes down, always available
4. **Cost** — Cheap or free to host and maintain long-term
5. **Aesthetics** — Makes my business look premium and trustworthy

**What they do NOT care about:** Which JavaScript framework was used, edge network architecture, GSAP animation internals, or system IDs. Technical implementation is invisible to them.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Faster. Bold. Creative.

**Voice & tone:** Confident but not arrogant. Direct but not cold. The tone should feel like a creative partner who *gets it* — someone who translates business goals into stunning digital experiences without making the client feel dumb for not knowing what "Astro" means.

**Emotional goal:** When a local business owner lands on VividStack, they should think: *"This agency is creative and will take care of my branding."* — not *"These people are great developers."* The craft should be felt, not explained.

**Copy principle — Show outcomes, not implementation:**
- ❌ "Astro v4.0 + Cloudflare Edge" → ✅ "Loads in under a second, anywhere in the world"
- ❌ "Enterprise architecture" → ✅ "Built to grow with your business"
- ❌ "Risk Architecture / Security Protocol" → ✅ "Insurance that looks trustworthy"
- ❌ "Core_System_01" → ✅ "Personal Brand Website"
- ❌ "Sub-millisecond dynamic routing via advanced edge network" → ✅ "Your site never goes down"

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Brutalist-tech — sharp edges, hard offset box-shadows, monospace accents, restrained color palette. This is the agency's chosen identity and should be preserved. The brutalism communicates boldness and confidence; the tech overlay communicates precision.

**What this should NOT look like:** A cheap, generic design agency. No stock photo grids, no pastel gradients, no rounded-corner card soup, no "we're just like the other 10,000 agencies" templates. The design itself is the portfolio — it should feel handcrafted and opinionated.

**Theme:** Light mode only. The navy (`#253644`) and cream (`#F8F9FA`) palette is the permanent direction. Dark mode is not on the roadmap.

**References:** None specified — the current design IS the reference. Preserve and refine it.

**Legal pages:** `/privacy` and `/terms` use the same typographic system (Space Grotesk headings, Inter body, mint section label, brutalist focus rings) on `bg-vivid-cream` for consistency with the marketing site.

## Component Patterns

### Brutalist Card
```css
border: 2px solid #000;
box-shadow: 8px 8px 0 0 #000;
/* Hover: */
box-shadow: 12px 12px 0 0 #000;
transform: translateY(-4px);
```

Tailwind also exposes scaled shadows (e.g. `shadow-brutalist`, `shadow-brutalist-lg`, neon and navy variants) — see `tailwind.config.mjs`.

### Glass Panel (Process section)
```css
background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.6);
backdrop-filter: blur(12px);
border-radius: 1.5rem;
```
Applied via `.glass-panel` in `Process.astro`.

### Section Label (above many headings)
```html
<span class="text-vivid-mint font-bold uppercase tracking-[0.4em] text-sm mb-4 block">
  Label Text
</span>
```
Legal pages use a slightly tighter `tracking-[0.35em]` — keep that page type consistent.

### Shared buttons
Brutalist primary/secondary patterns live as `.btn-brutalist-primary` and `.btn-brutalist-secondary` in `global.css` (Montserrat, min-height 48px, reduced-motion hover rules).

## Animation Conventions

- **Easing:** `power2.out` for scroll-triggered, `power4.out` for page load (GSAP)
- **Duration:** 0.9–1.4s entrances, 0.3s micro-interactions
- **Stagger:** 0.1–0.2s between siblings
- **Desktop gate:** Floating/orbit-style loops and Lenis smooth scrolling target viewports `>= 1024px` at initialization (see `home-motion.ts`)
- **Reduced motion:** When `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` is active, use opacity-first simplification; avoid transform-heavy and floating motion. Buttons drop hover translate in CSS (`global.css`).

## Design Principles

### 1. Outcomes Over Implementation
The user is a local business owner, not a developer. Every heading, label, and description should answer "what does this do for ME?" not "what technology powers this?" Technical excellence should be *felt* through the site's speed and polish, never *explained* through jargon.

### 2. The Site Is the Portfolio
VividStack's own website is the strongest sales pitch. Every interaction, animation, and layout decision must demonstrate the agency's creative capability. If the site doesn't wow, no amount of copy will convince a client.

### 3. Bold, Not Busy
Brutalism done right is about confident restraint — strong typography, decisive whitespace, and hard shadows that feel intentional. Never add decorative elements that don't serve the narrative. Every pixel earns its place.

### 4. Mobile Is the Real Homepage
Local business clients and their customers live on phones. The mobile experience isn't a scaled-down afterthought — it's the primary viewport. Instagram and TikTok traffic will land on mobile first. Design for 375px, then scale up.

### 5. Fast by Default
Performance is a feature, not a metric. Every image is optimized, every animation is GPU-accelerated where used, every layout avoids unnecessary reflows. Defer non-critical scripts (motion bundle, Web3Forms). The site should feel instant — that speed itself builds client trust ("if their site is this fast, mine will be too").

## Accessibility

- **Target:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- **Color contrast:** All text must meet 4.5:1 (normal) / 3:1 (large) contrast ratios
- **Reduced motion:** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` — GSAP instant state + CSS button/transition adjustments
- **Focus states:** All interactive elements must have visible focus indicators (e.g. rings on legal links, form controls, skip link)
- **Semantic HTML:** Proper heading hierarchy, landmark regions (`main`, `nav`), alt text on images
- **Touch targets:** Minimum 44×44px on mobile (footer legal links, buttons)
