# VividStack — Complete Marketing Strategy & Action Plan

## Business Context

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **What you sell** | Single-page, multi-page, and CMS websites for small/local businesses |
| **Core differentiators** | Built to convert (not just pretty), hosting included, fast delivery, guided process |
| **Target audience** | Local businesses without a good website — restaurants, salons, trades, clinics, studios, consultants, insurance agencies, etc. |
| **Current proof** | 4 portfolio projects across niches (brand, corporate, insurance, photography) |
| **Lead capture** | Contact form on single-page site (no pricing visible — conversation-driven) |

---

## Strategy Philosophy

> **Social-first storytelling.** New agencies don't compete on authority — they compete on **visibility** and **proof**. Every piece of content should do one of:
> 1. **Show your work** (before/after, process breakdowns)
> 2. **Educate your buyer** (why website speed matters, what good copy looks like)
> 3. **Build trust** (client testimonials, behind-the-scenes, quick wins)

---

## 1. Platform Strategy

### Primary Platform: Instagram (daily)
Best for visual work. Design agencies naturally perform here.

| Content Type | Frequency | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Before/After reveals | 2-3x/week | Reel or carousel |
| Quick tips (website design, SEO, copy) | 2x/week | Reel (30-60s talking over screen recording) |
| Process behind-the-scenes | 1x/week | Reel (screen recording of you building, with commentary) |
| Client wins / testimonials | 1x/week | Carousel or single post |
| Portfolio project spotlight | 1x/week | Carousel with 5-7 slides breaking down the design decisions |

**Profile optimization:**
- Bio: "Websites for local businesses that turn visitors into customers. Hosting included. Free consultation → [link]"
- Link: vividaestack.com/#contact
- Stories highlights: Services, Process, Portfolio, Reviews, FAQ
- Post consistently at 8-9 AM or 6-7 PM (local time) when business owners scroll

---

### Secondary Platform: Facebook (3-4x/week)

Local businesses AND their customers are on Facebook. Dual-purpose: show expertise + run ads.

**Organic:**
- Cross-post Instagram Reels to Facebook Reels (same content, double distribution)
- Join 10-15 local business groups in your target area (e.g., "Small Business Owners of [City]", "Local Entrepreneurs Network")
- Post value-first content in groups: "5 things every restaurant website needs" (not "Hire me")
- Comment helpfully on people asking about websites in those groups

**Facebook Page:**
- Mirror Instagram content but add more text context
- Pin a "How Our 4-Step Process Works" post to the top
- Set up Reviews tab and ask every happy client to leave one

---

### Tertiary Platform: TikTok (3-4x/week)

TikTok's algorithm is your best friend for organic reach as a new account.

| Content Type | Hook Example |
|---|---|
| "I redesigned a local business website in X minutes" | "This bakery's website loaded in 4 seconds. I fixed it to 0.8. Here's what changed." |
| "Website mistakes killing your business" | "3 things on your website making customers leave (I checked 12 local business sites)" |
| Before/After transformations | Screen recording with before → after zoom reveal |
| "How much does a website actually cost?" | Breaking down agency vs. freelancer vs. DIY costs transparently |
| Day in the life / process | "Come watch me build a dental clinic website" |
| Industry-specific tips | "If you own a salon, your website needs these 3 pages" |

**TikTok-specific tactics:**
- Use trending audio on reveal videos (boosts algorithmic distribution)
- Add captions — 80% watch muted
- Keep videos 15-45 seconds max (retention matters more than depth)
- Reply to comments with video replies (free content, shows engagement)
- Post at 6-10 PM when people are scrolling

---

### Platform: LinkedIn (2x/week)

B2B-adjacent businesses on LinkedIn (consultants, insurance agencies, corporate). Lower volume but higher-intent leads.

- Post case studies as long-form posts
- Share "here's the math on why a slow website costs you customers" type content
- Comment on posts by small business leaders in your area
- Connect with local chamber of commerce members, BNI groups

## 2. Content Pillars (The 4 Buckets)

Every post should fall into one of these four:

### Pillar 1: Show (40% of content)
- Before/after transformations
- Portfolio breakdowns ("why I chose this layout for a restaurant")
- Live builds / screen recordings
- Client project walkthroughs

### Pillar 2: Teach (30%)
- "Why your website needs these 5 sections"
- "What SEO actually means in plain English"
- "Hosting explained in 60 seconds"
- "The difference between a website and a web presence"
- "How to take good photos for your business website"

### Pillar 3: Proof (15%)
- Client testimonials (video > text)
- Case studies with numbers ("went from 0 to 12 leads/week")
- Screenshots of positive feedback
- Milestone posts ("just shipped our 10th client site")

### Pillar 4: Personality (15%)
- Behind the scenes of your workflow
- "What I learned from building X websites"
- Opinion/hot takes ("Your $500 Wix site is costing you more")
- Day-in-the-life content

## 3. Lead Generation Funnel

```
Social Content (Top of Funnel)
       ↓
Profile Link → vividaestack.com
       ↓
Portfolio + Services Page (build trust)
       ↓
Contact Form (no pricing = conversation starter)
       ↓
Discovery Call / DM Conversation
       ↓
Close Client
       ↓
```

### Funnel Optimization

- **Instagram/TikTok bio link**: Point directly to `#contact` or create a simple `/free-consultation` landing page
- **Link in every post**: "Link in bio for a free website review"
- **CTA on every 3rd post**: "DM me 'WEBSITE' and I'll review yours for free"
- **Stories CTA**: Use the link sticker to drive to contact form
- **Pinned comment**: On every TikTok/Reel, pin a comment with your CTA

## 6. Growth Hacks

### The "Free Website Review" Funnel
- Post: "Send me your website URL and I'll record a 60-second video with 3 things to fix. Free. DM me."
- Record a Loom video reviewing their current site
- Send them the Loom link + "Want me to fix these? Reply YES"
- This is your #1 lead magnet

### Local SEO Play
- Create a Google Business Profile for VividStack
- Ask every client for a Google review
- Post "web designer in [city]" content on your socials
- This captures people actively searching, not just social scrollers

### The "Build in Public" Strategy
- Post daily progress on building a website (time-lapse)
- People love watching work get created — it's inherently satisfying
- End with: "Want one built? Link in bio"

### Referral Incentive
- "Refer a business owner who books with us, and both of you get [X] free"
- Could be: free monthly update, 1 month hosting free, or a small gift card
- Your existing clients are your best source of new clients

### Collaboration Posts
- Partner with complementary businesses: photographers, copywriters, marketers
- Cross-post content to each other's audiences
- "We built this site, [photographer] did the photos — tag team"

## 11. The 30-60-90 Day Plan

### Days 1-30: Foundation
- Post consistently on IG + TikTok (20+ pieces)
- Cross-post to Facebook (minimum 10 pieces)
- Create all 5 Story highlights
- Get 3+ client testimonials
- Test 1 Facebook/IG ad at $5/day
- Establish content pillars and see what resonates
- **Target**: 5-10 qualified inquiries

### Days 31-60: Optimize + Scale
- Double down on top 3 content types (the ones that got most engagement/inquiries)
- Increase ad spend to $10-15/day if ROI positive
- Start the "free website review" funnel as a system
- Launch referral program
- Post your first LinkedIn case study
- **Target**: 15-20 inquiries, 3-5 closed clients

### Days 90+: Authority
- You now have proof of concept — scale what works
- Add YouTube Shorts (same content repurposed)
- Consider a podcast or longer YouTube content ("Building websites for local businesses" series)
- Launch email newsletter with monthly website tips
- Partner with local business associations
- **Target**: 30+ inquiries/month, 5-8 clients/month
